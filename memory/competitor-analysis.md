## Recommendations

1. **"Build in public" your outreach-agent** — Post the actual terminal output, the actual pipeline results, the actual code. "This agent just found 20 leads, enriched their emails, and sent personalized cold emails in 4 minutes. Here's the run." Nobody else can do this because they're not builders.

2. **Counter-position ColdIQ's $6M ARR story** — "ColdIQ charges $5K/mo to do what this open-source agent does for free. Here's a side-by-side." Controversial, engagement-farming, AND it positions your tool.

3. **"Outbound for founders, not SDRs"** — Jason Bay talks to sales leaders. Suprava talks to email operators. ColdIQ talks to agency builders. Nobody talks to the technical founder who just wants pipeline without hiring an SDR or paying an agency. That's your ICP AND your content angle.

4. **Weekly "pipeline report" posts** — Share real numbers: "This week's outreach-agent run: 60 leads sourced, 42 emails verified, 38 sent, 6 replies, 2 calls booked. Total cost: $3.20 in API credits." Transparency + results + tool demo in one post.

5. **Hot take: "The outbound agency model is dead"** — All 3 competitors ARE agencies. You're building the thing that replaces them. Lean into it. Polarizing take + proof + free tool = maximum engagement.

## Creative Gap Analysis

### What's Working (relatively) for You
- **Comic illustration** got your best engagement (2 reactions). Visual storytelling > raw terminal footage.
- **Terminal recordings** are unique — nobody else in this space does them. But they're too "inside baseball" for LinkedIn scrollers.

### What's NOT Working
1. **Terminal videos look intimidating** — JSON output, API calls, code. Non-technical viewers scroll past. Even technical viewers need a reason to stop.
2. **No human element** — Your face isn't in any creative. Suprava uses memes (relatable), Jason uses his face in banner, Michel uses team headshots. LinkedIn is a people platform.
3. **No branded template** — Suprava has her dark carousel style. ColdIQ has their salmon event banners. You have no consistent visual identity.
4. **Link previews kill engagement** — LinkedIn deprioritizes posts with external links. Your GitHub/Loom link preview posts get the worst distribution.

| Tactic | Who Does It | How You Adapt It |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Meme format** | Suprava (her top performer by 3x) | Make cold outreach memes from a BUILDER perspective. "Types of outbound tools I've replaced with 10 lines of code" with escalating meme. |
| **Results screenshots** | ColdIQ (revenue milestones) | Screenshot your terminal showing "38 emails sent, 6 replies" — but add an annotation layer with arrows + context. Not raw terminal — annotated terminal. |
| **Comic illustrations** | YOU (already doing this) | Double down. This was your best post. AI-generated comics showing the before/after of manual outbound vs agent outbound. |
| **Bold text on solid color** | Suprava (rage bait) | Simple image: solid black background, white text, one provocative stat. "I spent $0 on outbound tools last month. I sent 400 emails." |

### Creative Playbook — Your Next 5 Visuals

1. **Annotated terminal screenshot** — Your agent running, but with bright arrows + callout boxes explaining what's happening. "Finding leads...", "Verifying emails...", "Sending personalized email #23...". Makes it accessible to non-technical viewers.

2. **Meme: "Types of outbound setups"** — Escalating brain meme or headache meme. Level 1: "Manually finding leads on LinkedIn" / Level 2: "Paying $500/mo for 6 SaaS tools" / Level 3: "Hiring a $5K/mo agency" / Level 4 (glowing brain): "One Claude Code command"

3. **Comic strip: Before vs After** — Left panels: founder drowning in Apollo, Clay, Instantly tabs. Right panels: same founder, one terminal, sipping coffee while agent runs. Use AI illustration.

4. **Bold stat card** — Black background, huge white text: "$0 outbound stack. 400 emails. 6 calls booked." Small subtitle: "open source. link in comments."

5. **Side-by-side cost comparison** — Clean infographic: "ColdIQ agency: $5K/mo" vs "Outreach Agent: $3.20/mo in API credits". Visual table format with check marks. Controversial but undeniable.
